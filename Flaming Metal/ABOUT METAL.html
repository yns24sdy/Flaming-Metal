Heavy metal, a powerful subgenre of rock, emerged in the late 1960s and early 1970s, characterized by distorted guitar tones, complex compositions, and aggressive rhythms. Pioneered by iconic bands like Black Sabbath, Metallica, and Iron Maiden, metal evolved into diverse subgenres including thrash metal, death metal, and power metal, often exploring themes of mythology, social critique, and philosophy. The genre demands high technical proficiency, particularly in guitar shredding and double-bass drumming. Beyond music, metal represents a cultural movement with distinctive visual aesthetics and a devoted global following. Continually innovating, metal remains a vital force influencing countless other musical styles today.
